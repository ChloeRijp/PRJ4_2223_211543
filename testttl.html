It's impossible to get anything done without using the internet nowadays. Yet, humans regard material goods highly. Possibilities for fond recollections of our youth. Even if everything is eventually done online, there's no reason to deny future generations the joy of this experience.

It is essential that children do more than just look at and take home advertising materials like catalogs; they should also engage in imaginative and interactive activities with them. Consumers' time spent at a store can be improved by giving them the opportunity to engage in a fun activity through promotional materials or catalogs.

Our campaign will give kids a chance to feel like they belong in the Lego world thanks to the personalized catalog with engaging features they've asked for. Children will be more likely to pick up the catalog and peruse it if they are encouraged to participate in a treasure hunt for a golden Lego piece that will unlock the reward of a free, fully-customizable Minifigure.

Because of the lightning-fast pace at which technology is evolving, even the cutting-edge strategy we've developed to get young people to pick up print catalogs may soon be outdated.

While our software and hardware installation process is straightforward, problems can arise regularly. Defects may be difficult to fix, leading to customer unhappiness and a less than satisfied customer base among the kids who need adult assistance in order to receive their own individualized catalog.
